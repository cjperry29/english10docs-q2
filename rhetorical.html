Introduction to Rhetoric and the Rhetorical Triangle

Rhetoric is - The act of persuasive language

Writers and speakers use rhetoric to convince readers and listeners to: Convince readers and listeners to do something or to think something

The 3 elements of the rhetorical triangle are:

  1. Speaker or Writer
  2. Audience
  3. Purpose

Rhetorical appeals are:

Appeal to Ethos:

Character or Authority

Trustworthyness or Credibility of Speaker

Dependable and Knowlagable

Appeal to Pathos:

Emotional

Desire, Passion, Patriotism

Within the Audience/Reader

Appeal to Logos:

Logic and Reason

Data and Research

Rhetorical Devices

Rhetorical devices are - used to achieve a desired affect

Alliteration - Repatition of the initial letter

Anaphora - The same expression is repeated from the beginning

Hyperbole - Exaggeration for emphasis

Understatement - For intesification
