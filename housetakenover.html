“House Taken Over” by Julio Cortazar

Comprehension Check

1. Briefly describe the house in which the narrator and his sister live.

It is big and spacious, it has been passed down throughout generations.

2. What is the source of the siblings’ income?

The siblings own a farm.

3. How do Irene and the narrator occupy their time?

Irene knits wool and the narrator reads French books.

4. What decision do Irene and the narrator make when they realize the back part of the house has been taken over?

They have to live on the front side of the house.

5. What happens to the brother and sister at the end of the story?

They are forced out to the street.

Analyze the Text

1.(a) Interpret- To what social class do the brother and sister belong? How do you know?

(b) Connect How are the characters’ hobbies evidence of their social class?

A. They live in the upper class because the narrator said that they did not have to work hard to earn a living. / B. Because they are laid back and do not worry about much of what will happen outside of their house.

2.(a) Compare and Contrast- How is the behavior the siblings exhibit during sleep different from their behavior while awake?

When they are awake they are careless but when they go to sleep they are restless.

(b) Analyze- What does this difference suggest about their true reactions to the invasion of the house?

They are surprised and active when they find out that the back park has been taken over.

3. What do the siblings do with the key? How is this symbolic?

They toss the key in the sewer. This is a symbol meaning they will not come back.

4. Who, or what, do you interpret the “they” to be in story?

5. Extend- Cortázar wrote this story after having a nightmare. In what ways does this story resemble a nightmare? Explain.

6. Essential Question: What is the allure of fear? What have you learned from this story about portrayals of fear in literature?
